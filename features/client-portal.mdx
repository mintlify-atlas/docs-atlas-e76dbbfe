## How the portal works

Every client in LWD Invoices has a unique `portalToken` that never changes. The portal is accessible at:

```
https://yourdomain.com/portal/<portalToken>
```

Share this URL with your client to give them access. No account, password, or invitation is required by default. You can optionally protect the portal with a passphrase (see [Passphrase protection](#passphrase-protection) below).

Each invoice also has its own `portalToken`, so you can share a direct link to a specific invoice without exposing the full client dashboard.

## Client dashboard

When a client visits their portal URL, they land on their **client dashboard**, which shows:

- A summary of their outstanding and overdue balance
- A complete list of all their invoices with statuses, amounts, and due dates
- Recent payment history
- Active projects shared with them (if any)

From the dashboard a client can click into any invoice to view details, make a payment, or leave a comment.

## Viewing and paying invoices

From any invoice in the portal, a client can:

- View the full invoice with all line items, taxes, and discounts
- See payment history for that invoice
- Pay the invoice online (if the invoice status is `SENT`, `PARTIALLY_PAID`, or `OVERDUE`)
- Pay a specific installment if the invoice has a partial payment schedule
- Leave a public comment visible to both staff and client

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Pay with Stripe">
    If Stripe is configured for your organization, a **Pay with card** button appears on payable invoices. Clicking it redirects the client to a Stripe Checkout session. After a successful payment, Stripe sends a webhook that automatically marks the invoice as paid and sends a receipt email.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Pay with PayPal">
    If PayPal is configured, a PayPal button is available on the invoice. The client completes payment through PayPal's standard flow. Webhook events update the invoice status automatically.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>
Gateway surcharges, if configured, are added to the checkout amount automatically. Clients see the final amount including any processing fee before confirming payment.
</Note>

## Approving and signing estimates

When you send an estimate or proposal, the client can review it in the portal and take one of two actions:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review the estimate">
    The client opens the portal link for the estimate and reads through the line items, pricing, and any attached proposal content.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Accept or reject">
    The client clicks **Accept** or **Decline**. Accepting moves the estimate to `ACCEPTED` status; declining moves it to `REJECTED`. Your team is notified immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign the proposal (if required)">
    If the estimate has proposal content attached, the client can provide an e-signature. They enter their full name and email address, then draw or type their signature.

    On submission:
    - The estimate status is set to `ACCEPTED`
    - The signature data is encrypted and stored
    - A `SignatureAuditLog` entry is created with SHA-256 hashes of both the document content and the signature
    - A notification and confirmation email are sent to your team
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once signed, a proposal cannot be signed again. The audit log entry provides a tamper-evident record for compliance.

## Project visibility

Staff can expose project information to clients on a per-project basis using two toggle options:

| Setting | What the client sees |
|---|---|
| Show project in portal | Project name, status, and due date on the client dashboard |
| Show timesheet | All time entries logged against the project |

These settings are independent — you can show a project without sharing the timesheet, or share both.

Milestones and tasks also have individual visibility flags. Only milestones and tasks marked as viewable appear in the client's project view.

## Passphrase protection

By default, anyone with the portal URL can access it. To add a layer of security:

1. Go to the client record and set a **portal passphrase**.
2. The passphrase is hashed with bcrypt before storage — it is never stored in plain text.
3. When the client visits their portal URL, they are prompted to enter the passphrase before a session is created.

<Warning>
If you set a passphrase, make sure to share it with your client securely. There is no password recovery flow — you would need to set a new passphrase if it is lost.
</Warning>

## Portal branding

Customize the portal's appearance to match your brand under **Settings → Organization**:

| Setting | Description |
|---|---|
| Logo | Your organization logo, displayed in the portal header |
| Brand color | Primary accent color for buttons and highlights |
| Brand font | Typography choice: Inter, Georgia, or system default |
| Portal tagline | Short text shown below the organization name |
| Footer text | Custom text displayed in the portal footer |
| Hide "Powered by" | Removes the LWD Invoices attribution from the portal footer |

## Invoice comments

Both staff and clients can leave comments on an invoice:

- **Client comments** are created through the portal and are always public (visible to both sides).
- **Staff comments** can be marked as **private** — private comments are never shown in the portal.
- When a client posts a comment, all admin users in your organization receive an in-app notification and an email alert.

## Session tracking

Each time a client creates a dashboard session (by visiting their portal URL), a `ClientPortalSession` record is created that stores:

- The session token (hashed, time-limited)
- The client's IP address
- The client's browser user agent
- The session expiry timestamp

Sessions expire automatically. If a session token is expired, the client is redirected to log in again (or re-enter their passphrase if one is set).
